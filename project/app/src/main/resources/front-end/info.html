pH
  • Measures how acidic or alkaline the water is (scale 0–14).
  • Below 7 is acidic, above 7 is alkaline.
  • Recommended safe drinking water range: 6.5 – 8.5.
  • Water that is too acidic (below 6.5) can corrode pipes and release harmful metals like lead and copper.
  • Water that is too alkaline (above 8.5) can cause scale build-up and reduce the effectiveness of chlorine disinfection.
Conductivity
  • Measures how well water conducts electricity.
  • Caused by dissolved salts, minerals, and ions in the water.
  • Measured in microsiemens per centimetre (µS/cm).
  • Typical safe range: 200 – 800 µS/cm.
  • Low conductivity (<200 µS/cm): may indicate a lack of essential minerals and can make water more corrosive.
  • High conductivity (>800 µS/cm): may indicate pollution from runoff or contamination.
