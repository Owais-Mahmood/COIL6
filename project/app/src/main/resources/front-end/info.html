Water Monitoring Dashboard

pH:
  • Measures how acidic or alkaline the water is, on a scale of 0 to 14.
  • Below 7 is acidic and above 7 is an alkaline.
  • The WHO (World Health Organisation) recommends drinking water sites between 6.5 and 8.5.
  • Water that is too acidic (below 6.5) can corrode pipes, allowing heavy metals like lead and copper into the water supply. This is extremely toxic for humans resulting in a sour taste and irritation of skin and eyes.
  • Water that is too alkaline (above 8.5) can cause scale build up in pipes. The water can taste bitter and there is a reduction in effectiveness of chlorine disinfection, meaning bacteria is less likeley to be killed.
Conductivity:
  • Measures how well the water can conduct electricity.
  • Pure water conducts electricity very poorly, it is the dissolved salts, mineral and ions in water that carry the electrical charge.
  • It is measured in microsiemens per centimetre.
  • For drinking water the acceptable range is 200-800 µS/cm.
  • When the conductivity is too low (below around 200 µS/cm) it indicates very few dissolved minerals, which sounds clean but can actually mean the water is too pure and lacks essential minerals like calcium and magnesium that the body needs. It can also indicate the water is more corrosive to pipes.
  • When the water is too high in conductivity (above around 800 µS/cm) it suggests a high concentration of dissolved solids, potentially from agricultural runoff, industrial waste, sewage contamination, or road salt. High conductivity water can taste salty or bitter and may contain harmful contaminants.
